Formula KE = 1/2 mv2

Kinetic energy is the energy of a moving object.

Mass m

Measured in kilograms. More mass means more energy.

Velocity v

Measured in meters per second. It is squared, so it matters a lot.

How to Calculate

  • KE = 1/2 mv^2
  • KE = 1/2(5)(6^2)
  • KE = 1/2(5)(36)
  • KE = 90 J

What to notice

  • Doubling mass doubles kinetic energy.
  • Doubling velocity makes kinetic energy four times larger.
  • An object at rest has zero kinetic energy.
